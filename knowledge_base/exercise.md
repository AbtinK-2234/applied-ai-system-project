# Pet Exercise and Enrichment Guide

## Dog Exercise

### Daily Exercise Requirements by Age
- **Puppies (under 1 year)**: 5 minutes of exercise per month of age, twice daily. A 3-month-old puppy needs about 15 minutes twice a day. Over-exercising puppies can damage developing joints.
- **Adult dogs (1-7 years)**: 30 minutes to 2 hours daily depending on breed and energy level
- **Senior dogs (7+ years)**: 20-30 minutes of gentle exercise daily. Shorter, more frequent walks are better than one long session. Adjust based on mobility and health conditions.

### Exercise by Breed Type
- **High-energy breeds** (Border Collie, Australian Shepherd, Husky, Jack Russell): 1-2 hours of vigorous exercise daily. Need both physical and mental stimulation.
- **Medium-energy breeds** (Labrador, Golden Retriever, Beagle): 45-90 minutes daily. Enjoy walks, fetch, and swimming.
- **Low-energy breeds** (Bulldog, Basset Hound, Shih Tzu): 20-40 minutes daily. Shorter walks and gentle play. Be cautious in hot weather, especially with brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds.

### Types of Dog Exercise
- **Walking**: The foundation of daily exercise. Vary routes for mental stimulation.
- **Running/Jogging**: Suitable for adult dogs of appropriate breeds. Build up distance gradually. Avoid in puppies and senior dogs.
- **Fetch**: Great cardiovascular exercise. Use soft toys on grass to protect joints.
- **Swimming**: Excellent low-impact exercise, especially for dogs with joint issues. Not all dogs are natural swimmers; introduce water gradually and always supervise.
- **Tug-of-war**: Good physical and mental exercise. Let the dog win sometimes.
- **Agility training**: Combines physical exercise with mental challenge. Start with low obstacles and work up.

### Mental Enrichment for Dogs
Physical exercise alone is not enough. Dogs need mental stimulation to prevent boredom and destructive behavior:
- **Puzzle toys**: Kong toys filled with treats, snuffle mats, treat-dispensing balls
- **Training sessions**: 10-15 minutes of obedience or trick training counts as mental exercise
- **Nose work**: Hide treats around the house or yard for your dog to find
- **Socialization**: Supervised playdates with other dogs
- **New experiences**: Different walking routes, new environments, car rides

## Cat Exercise and Enrichment

### Daily Exercise Needs
Indoor cats need at least 15-30 minutes of active play daily, split into multiple short sessions (cats prefer short bursts of activity). Without adequate play, indoor cats are prone to obesity, boredom, and behavioral problems.

### Types of Cat Play
- **Interactive toys**: Feather wands, laser pointers (always end with a physical toy the cat can catch), fishing rod toys. Interactive play mimics hunting behavior.
- **Solo toys**: Crinkle balls, catnip mice, spring toys, ball tracks. Rotate toys weekly to maintain interest.
- **Climbing and perching**: Cat trees, wall-mounted shelves, window perches. Cats need vertical space for exercise, observation, and security.
- **Puzzle feeders**: Slow feeders, treat balls, food puzzles. Making cats work for their food provides mental stimulation and slows eating.

### Environmental Enrichment for Cats
- **Window access**: Bird feeders outside windows provide visual stimulation
- **Hiding spots**: Boxes, paper bags (remove handles), cat tunnels
- **Scratching surfaces**: Multiple scratching posts and pads in different orientations (vertical and horizontal) and materials (sisal, cardboard)
- **Catnip and cat grass**: Safe plants that many cats enjoy
- **Rotation**: Change the layout and available toys periodically

## Exercise Safety Tips

### Weather Considerations
- **Hot weather**: Avoid exercise during peak heat (10am-4pm). Walk on grass instead of hot pavement (test with your hand - if too hot for you, it's too hot for paws). Watch for signs of heatstroke: excessive panting, drooling, glazed eyes, rapid heartbeat. Brachycephalic breeds are especially vulnerable.
- **Cold weather**: Short-haired and small dogs may need sweaters. Wipe paws after walks to remove ice, salt, and de-icing chemicals. Watch for frostbite on ears, tail, and paws.
- **Rain**: Many dogs enjoy rain walks with proper drying afterward. Avoid areas with standing water that may contain bacteria.

### Signs of Over-Exercise
- Excessive panting that does not resolve with rest
- Limping or reluctance to continue walking
- Lying down and refusing to move
- Muscle tremors
- Vomiting during or after exercise

### Exercise for Overweight Pets
Start slowly with 10-15 minutes of gentle walking, gradually increasing duration and intensity over weeks. Swimming is excellent for overweight dogs as it's low-impact. For cats, start with 5-minute play sessions and increase gradually. Always consult your veterinarian before starting an exercise program for an overweight pet.

## Scheduling Exercise

### Consistency is Key
Pets thrive on routine. Try to exercise at the same times each day. Morning exercise can help calm energetic dogs for the rest of the day. Evening play sessions help cats burn energy before bedtime (reducing nighttime activity).

### Balancing Exercise with Rest
Puppies and senior pets need more rest between activity sessions. Provide a quiet, comfortable resting area after exercise. Watch for signs that your pet needs a break - slowing down, seeking shade, or heavy panting.
