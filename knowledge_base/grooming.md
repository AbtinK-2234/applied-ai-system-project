# Pet Grooming Guide

## Dog Grooming

### Brushing
Regular brushing removes loose fur, distributes natural oils, and prevents matting. Frequency depends on coat type:
- **Short-haired breeds** (Beagle, Boxer): Brush once a week with a bristle brush or rubber grooming mitt
- **Medium-haired breeds** (Golden Retriever, Border Collie): Brush 2-3 times per week with a slicker brush and undercoat rake
- **Long-haired breeds** (Shih Tzu, Yorkshire Terrier): Brush daily with a pin brush and wide-tooth comb to prevent tangles and mats
- **Wire-haired breeds** (Schnauzer, Wire Fox Terrier): Brush 2-3 times per week; may need hand-stripping or professional grooming every 6-8 weeks
- **Double-coated breeds** (Husky, German Shepherd): Brush 2-3 times per week; daily during shedding season (spring and fall). Never shave a double-coated breed as the undercoat provides insulation.

### Bathing
Most dogs need bathing every 4-8 weeks, but this varies. Over-bathing strips natural oils and can cause dry skin. Use dog-specific shampoo (human shampoo has the wrong pH). Rinse thoroughly as residual shampoo causes irritation. Dogs with skin conditions may need medicated baths more frequently as prescribed by a veterinarian.

### Nail Trimming
Trim nails every 2-4 weeks. Long nails can cause pain, affect gait, and lead to joint problems. Use sharp nail clippers or a grinding tool. Avoid cutting the quick (blood vessel inside the nail). If the quick is cut, apply styptic powder to stop bleeding. Dark nails make the quick harder to see; trim small amounts at a time.

### Ear Cleaning
Clean ears every 1-2 weeks, especially for floppy-eared breeds prone to infections. Use a veterinarian-approved ear cleaning solution. Never insert anything into the ear canal. Signs of ear problems include odor, discharge, redness, swelling, and head shaking.

### Dental Care
Brush teeth daily or at minimum 3 times per week using pet-specific toothpaste (never human toothpaste, which contains xylitol and fluoride). Start slowly, letting your dog get used to the taste and sensation. Dental chews and toys can supplement brushing but do not replace it.

## Cat Grooming

### Brushing
Cats are generally good self-groomers, but brushing helps reduce hairballs and shedding:
- **Short-haired cats**: Brush once a week with a fine-tooth comb or rubber brush
- **Long-haired cats** (Persian, Maine Coon): Brush daily to prevent mats, especially behind ears, under legs, and around the tail
- **Senior cats**: May need more frequent brushing as they groom themselves less

### Bathing
Most cats rarely need baths. Exceptions include cats that get into something dirty or sticky, cats with skin conditions, and hairless breeds (Sphynx) that need weekly baths to remove oil buildup. Use lukewarm water and cat-specific shampoo. Keep water out of ears and eyes.

### Nail Trimming
Trim cat nails every 2-3 weeks. Use cat-specific nail clippers. Trim only the transparent tip, avoiding the pink quick. If your cat resists, try trimming one or two nails at a time during calm moments. Scratching posts help maintain nail health between trims.

### Ear and Eye Care
Check ears weekly for wax buildup, odor, or discharge. Clean with a cotton ball dampened with vet-approved ear cleaner. For eye discharge, gently wipe with a damp cotton ball from the inner corner outward. Persistent discharge warrants a vet visit.

## Grooming as Health Monitoring

Regular grooming sessions are an excellent opportunity to check for:
- **Lumps and bumps**: New growths that should be evaluated by a vet
- **Skin irritation**: Redness, flaking, hot spots, or sores
- **Parasites**: Fleas (look for flea dirt - tiny black specks), ticks (especially around ears, neck, and between toes)
- **Coat changes**: Dull coat, excessive shedding, or bald patches can indicate nutritional deficiencies or health issues
- **Dental problems**: Bad breath, red gums, loose teeth
- **Weight changes**: Noticeable weight gain or loss

## Grooming Schedule Recommendations

### Minimum Weekly Grooming Tasks
1. Full body brush (5-15 minutes depending on coat)
2. Check ears, eyes, and teeth
3. Check paws and nails
4. Look for any skin abnormalities

### Monthly Grooming Tasks
1. Nail trim
2. Ear cleaning
3. Bath (if needed)
4. Check and clean teeth thoroughly

### Professional Grooming
Consider professional grooming every 4-8 weeks for breeds that require haircuts or specialized care. Professional groomers can also express anal glands, perform thorough deshedding, and identify skin issues you might miss.
